Himekaji Fashion

also known as "Casual Princess"

Himekaji is a Japanese fashion style popularized in the 2000s-2010s. When translated literally, it means "casual princess" and it is derived from the "himegyaru," or "princess gyaru" style.

Himegyaru is a substyle of the larger gyaru fashion subculture. Gyaru is a Japanese transliteration of the English sland word "gal." It's an extravagant and flashy style that originated in the 1970's among young girls as a rebellion against harsh Japanese beauty standards. Though many substyles exist, in general gyaru's can be seen with bold makeup, extravagant hairstyles, and tanned skin.

Himekaji is a toned down version of himegyaru. Characteristics of this style include a softer color palette with shades of cream and pink as well as ruffles and lace; basically, just think what a girly princess might wear on her day to day! Though himekaji outfits can be made with pieces from anywhere, I lean towards pieces from Liz Lisa (particularly their 2010s items), Axes Femme, and L'est Rose.
