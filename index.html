Himekaji Fashion

Himekaji is a Japanese fashion style popularized in the 2000s-2010s. When translated literally, it means "casual princess" and it is derived from the "himegyaru," or "princess gyaru" style.

Himekaji is a toned down version of himegyaru. Characteristics of this style include a softer color palette with shades of cream and pink as well as ruffles and lace; basically, just think what a girly princess might wear on her day to day!

Key Terms

Co-ord

A complete outfit put together with a specific (typically Jfashion) style or aesthetic.

Gyaru

Transliteration of "gal." Japanese street fashion subculture originating in the 1970's as a rebellion against traditional beauty standards. It's an extravagant and flashy style that originated in the 1970's among young girls as a rebellion against harsh Japanese beauty standards. Though many substyles exist, in general gyaru's can be seen with bold makeup, extravagant hairstyles, and tanned skin.

Lolita

No, it has nothing to do with the novel by Vladamir Nabokov. Similar to Gyaru Fashion, the Lolita style is a fashion subculture originating in the 1970's. The movement was about dressing for yourself rather than others. Inspired by Rococo and Victorian aesthetics, Lolita fashion often features poofy bell-shaped or a-line skirts, an abudance of frills and ruffles, and extravagant accessories including but not limited to bonnets, gloves, tights, and parasols. Substyles such as Sweet Lolita and Hime Lolita have many similarities to Himegyaru; however, Lolita tends to lean more modest.

OP

Abbreviation of "one piece." Refers to single piece dress, often in the context of Lolita fashion.
